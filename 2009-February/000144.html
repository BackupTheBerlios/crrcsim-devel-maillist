Hi Jens,

many thanks for your clear words. I agree to all points you stated in both
your quality related emails, and I hope that we can continue improving CRRCsim
with these points on our minds.

I hope that it's not necessary to switch to the "gatekeeper" development
model, allthough hg makes it easy for contributors to prepare patches
or export whole changesets and send them to the gatekeeper instead of
committing directly. The "gatekeeper" model puts some extra load on the
shoulders of the core team, because they have to review every incoming
piece of code. And as you already said, our time for CRRCsim is very
limited.

Well, we often talked about coding style and "best practice" development,
but I guess it's time to write it down on a wiki page. Everything was
written down in different emails, some knowledge is retained in the
astyle and indent command lines in the old Makefiles. But I should write
it down in the wiki to have it all in one place.

But before, I think that we should get 0.9.10 on the road. I hope that I can
take a look at your changes later.
